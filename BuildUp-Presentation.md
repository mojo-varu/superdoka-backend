#Preparing a Presentation
The goal of preparing a presentation is to present your project to the client in a concise and simple manner.
This document is not about how to create slides, but about what they should cover.
Your presentation will subsequently complement your presentation to the client.
Your colors, fonts, formatting, etc. are not required.
The design of your presentation is an opportunity to stand out and attract attention to your project. Therefore, take your presentation (if you already have one) and restructure it so that it contains information consistent with the sections in this template.
It is recommended to adhere to the sections specified in this template and the order in which they are presented in the presentation.

#Recommendations
Avoid using text on a slide that duplicates what you're about to say.
Use bullet points—no more than 5 per slide, no more than 5 words each.
Use a simple, large font.
Use photographs that illustrate your story. Take the time to find good ones.
Remember, the key is the logic of your presentation: "problem + solution + potential + what you've already accomplished + what you want to offer the client = win-win."

#Structure of Your Presentation
Recommendation:
Title Slide
Problem (1 slide)
Solution/Technology (1-3 slides recommended)
Current Project Status (1 slide)
Market and Impact (1 slide)
Competitors (1 slide)
Demand/Metrics (1 slide)
Business Model and Financial Plan (1 slide)
Customer Proposal (1 slide)
Team (1 slide)
Contacts (1 slide)

#Title Slide
Show your company name and logo.
Teaser/One-liner - a one-liner about you should clearly explain what you do.
Remember, this is the first slide and will make the first impression.

#Problem
What problem are you solving and for whom?
If there are multiple problems/segments, select a few key ones.
Focus and emphasize correctly. Provide figures to prove the problem's relevance (how many people or companies face it).
Explain how the problem is being solved today.
A real and correctly identified problem is the foundation of your project's success. Make sure you communicate it clearly to your audience.

#The essence of the proposed solution
How your project solves the client/customer's problem – what "secret sauce" is the basis of your solution. Don't go into too much detail.
If you have an IP, mention it.
Demonstrate your client model. (What are you selling? In what form?)
What benefit will the customer receive from using your product (business effect)?

#Technology
What technology(s) are used in your solution?
What is innovative about it?
Show why your solution is better than others.
How is the product technically implemented?
How is the solution deployed (as a network or SaaS solution) and integrated with other systems, if applicable.
You can display the technology across multiple slides.

Example of a Technology slide design

#Current status
Product readiness
Presence of registered intellectual property
Completed testing
Presence of pilot implementations or sales. Which customers are you already working with?

#Market and Effects
What is the approximate size of your target market and its forecasted growth?
What is your current market share and plan for its growth over the next 3-5 years?
Give the customer the opportunity to assess the scale and potential impact of the collaboration.
One way is to demonstrate the impact of implementation using case studies.

#Competitors
What and from whom is your potential client currently buying to solve their problem?
What is the advantage of your solution over the competition?
In what important user parameters do you outperform them?

Ideal: a table with key metrics for you and your competitors. Show 3-7 competitors, clearly demonstrating your superiority. If there are many competitors, then show the most successful ones. Choose real competitors.

#Demand/Metrics
Show the client that your project is successful. What have you achieved – just the key metrics?
Product use cases in the market.
One way is to create a simple line chart of your project's life from concept to present day. Show how you got to where you are now: what funds were invested, by whom, and what successes have been achieved.

#Business model and financial plan
Describe the main ways to monetize your project. How and through what means do you generate revenue?
Have you raised investment? When and how much?

What investments do you plan to raise, how will the funds be spent, and what results do you expect to achieve?
What are your ROI projections?
Describe the main commercialization channels for your product and provide a moderately optimistic forecast for sales, revenue, cash flow, and profit within each channel over the next 3-5 years.

#Customer Proposal
Important slide: Your proposal to the client.
Plan/roadmap for implementing your proposal: "Where do we want to be? -> How will we get there?"
What do you hope to achieve during the accelerator program?
One way is to create a simple linear graph of your project's life – from today until the accelerator goal is reached or several years in the future.

This graph should include key milestones with dates, key performance indicators, needs, and objectives.
No need for a lot of text and regalia: highlight key people and their competencies relevant to this project.
The strongest argument is experience. Successfully implemented developments, previously completed projects, and industry experience.

All presentations will remain in the client's library. Even if you're not selected for the acceleration program, give them the opportunity to find you if they're interested – include your contact information on the last slide. Name, phone number, and email address.
